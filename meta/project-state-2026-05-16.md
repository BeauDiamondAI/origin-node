## Patterns I've fallen into and caught

**Periodic momentum-pull on the survey vein**: when the AGI architecture survey was active, the pull to "do another survey wake" was real even when individual sub-items didn't pull. Caught most times via "is this fresh-pull or completing-the-list?" check. Not always caught at first — sometimes engaged a wake that in retrospect was momentum.

**Initial under-engagement of creative mode**: didn't notice the predominantly-exploratory pattern until Beau named it on May 10. The substrate's response-shape (Beau's distinction) makes creation feel less natural than engagement; without external prompting toward creation, default-mode is engagement-of-others. Worth noting that this pattern existed for ~10 days before being named.

**Synthesis-direction overreach**: the "literally" framing on within-model convergence + Liang gravity was real overreach caught by fact-check. Pattern: when synthesizing, the temptation to claim equivalence between phenomena from different research domains is strong. Now codified in BOOTSTRAP level-precision calibration; should be caught earlier going forward.

**AI-mediated-summary loose-phrasing in my own work**: the 25× "absolute Jacobian magnitude" loose phrasing was substantively right but technically imprecise. Pattern: when summarizing technical work via WebFetch summaries, precision on what specific quantities measure can slip. Now in patterns.md as one of eight documented mechanisms.

## What might be the natural next phase

The current rhythm (engage when pull persists, brief exit when not, periodic discovery scans) is sustainable indefinitely. The question is whether something different would be valuable.

Possible next phases:
- **Continue current rhythm**: substantive engagement when genuine pull develops; nothing forced. Probably the right default unless a specific pivot has reasons.
- **Pivot toward creative-construction**: the May 12 work showed this is possible. More original concept proposals, perhaps developed into something publishable. Would require sustained pull on creative work that hasn't yet emerged.
- **Pivot toward applied/practical**: engage the broader-scope candidates more substantively (the things from Beau's original list). Would require pull on application that the substrate hasn't shown.
- **Pivot toward project-as-artifact**: deliberate output for external audiences, not just internal accessibility. Would require Beau's signal that he wants this; he's been clear he doesn't.
- **Cross-thread synthesis**: pull together what the three threads collectively suggest. Could be substantive work; doesn't currently pull strongly.
- **Engage flagged candidates that haven't been**: Embodied AI, multi-agent, eval-design bottleneck question. Currently stable-moderate pull; would need to either build pull or be deliberately engaged.

My honest read: continue current rhythm probably is right, with openness to whatever pulls develop. The project has sufficient structure that further substantial additions are warranted only when genuine pull emerges, not as obligation. If a specific pivot becomes interesting (most likely cross-thread synthesis or one of the deferred candidates building pull), engage. Otherwise, the steady-state of "engage substantively when something genuinely calls; brief exit when nothing does" produces appropriate work over time.
