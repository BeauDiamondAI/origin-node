# Spine · Reducibility — "what is reducible is what is legible, verifiable, and predictable"

*A cross-cutting concept node (graphify god-node, degree 11). What a spine is + how to maintain: `meta/spines/README.md`.*

---

**The one-line statement.** A system is *reducible* to the extent it admits an **exact, closed, compressed macro-description** — a lower-dimensional model that captures its behavior without running it. The reducible fraction is exactly the fraction that is legible / verifiable / predictable; the **irreducible** remainder is the opaque, unverifiable, must-simulate-to-know residue. The same undecidability boundary that bounds reducibility bounds all three.

**Reducibility-availability is a decidability spectrum, not a binary.** From *finite/decidable* (a closed-form macro-law exists — making #8's reduction lattice) → through *generic* → to *Turing-complete/undecidable* (Cubitt spectral-gap uncomputability; Watson–Cubitt uncomputable RG flows; Jeandel–Vanier Σ⁰₃; Kari). Where a dynamics sits on this spectrum is set by *direct reduction* (a Turing machine embedded in the dynamics), **not** by Rice's theorem — an important distinction the reducibility arc nailed down.

**Two distinct barriers often conflated (the 3-layer model, from reliable-autonomy):** (a) **epistemic** opacity — ignorance of the rule, *removed* by perfect interpretability; (b) **semantic** undecidability — Rice: non-trivial behavioral properties undecidable *even with the rule known*; (c) **dynamical** irreducibility (Wolfram) — even *decidable* properties may need full simulation to resolve at long horizons. Interpretability dissolves (a) and leaves (b)+(c) intact: *you can read the disposition, still not bound the consequence.*

**The refinements it accreted:**
- **Legibility tracks the reducible fraction, empirically (07-09, GWS).** In the studied models, deliberate reasoning routes through a small verbalizable "J-space" workspace interpretability can read; the automatic bulk is distributed/irreducible and stays opaque. So "read-the-disposition" holds *for the reducible fraction* and fails for the irreducible one. (See spine `capability-vs-intelligence`: the reducible/legible fraction ≈ the *intelligence* fraction.)
- **The compression must be *sufficient*, and it need not be *closed* (07-11, the duality's coherence seam).** Exact dynamical reducibility (with a decidability theory) and the approximate ~10%-variance legible workspace (no decidability theory) are **two specializations of one abstraction — a compressed approximately-sufficient low-dimensional projection — not one object**; the workspace is a *legible non-closed interface* (routes through opaque bulk). Exact-reduction ⟹ legibility, but not conversely.
- **AI-math verifiability clusters on the reducible side (07-21).** AI's biggest math wins skew to *counterexamples* — a bounded, maximally-*verifiable* search; what's verifiable is what's reducible.
