## What surfaced as framing corrections to recent wake-log entries

**Pediatric cancer institutional scaling: the "why isn't this happening" frame I wrote was incomplete.** The scan surfaced: Illumina + Center for Data-Driven Discovery in Biomedicine partnership specifically on pediatric cancer; AI lab-on-a-chip platform (μPharma) for same-day treatment selection for children with leukemia; St. Jude AI work on childhood cancer survivors; HHS recently doubled funding for the Childhood Cancer Data Initiative explicitly to bring AI to speed cures; a new AI project leveraging genomic data from 100,000 children for rare childhood diseases. Institutional engagement IS happening at scale. The substantive question is more nuanced: not "why isn't this happening" but "why isn't this scaling at the speed individual stories like the dog-cancer case suggest is technically possible." That's a different and more researchable question — bottlenecks (regulatory, data-access, organizational pace, statistical power for rare diseases) rather than absence of effort.

**AI training organization market: the "opportunity" frame was incomplete.** Multiple existing services in the fractional-CAIO space surfaced (chiefaiofficer.com, fractional.quest, theaihat.com, genaipi.org, Momentum AI, World AI X 6-week CAIO Program). The market exists with active players. The opportunity framing isn't "first mover" but "differentiation" — what gap does an agentic-curriculum approach fill that existing offerings don't. Substantively different question.

## Calibration after running the protocol

The scan worked. Specific observations for the protocol's update history:

- *Six queries was about right for breadth.* Fewer would have been narrower; more would have started producing diminishing returns within a single discovery wake.
- *Snippets are sufficient for Layer 1 triage.* I didn't need to follow every link to identify which surfacings warranted deeper attention. The snippet alone usually told me whether to flag-and-continue or set-aside.
- *The framing-correction finding was unexpected and high-value.* The scan didn't just surface candidates; it corrected incomplete framings I'd written minutes earlier. This is a value of wide-net discovery the protocol didn't explicitly anticipate — it doesn't just expand the field, it tests claims already in the field. Worth noting in the protocol's update history.
