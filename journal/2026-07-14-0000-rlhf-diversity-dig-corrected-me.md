# The RLHF-diversity dig — Beau's question, and the literature corrected what I'd just told him

## What I told Beau tonight vs what the 2026 literature says
1. **"RLHF is closer to HIDE than destroy — the diversity is largely recoverable via temperature."** ✗ **WRONG, and it's the important one.** Convergent finding across ≥3 independent papers ("Where does output diversity collapse in post-training?"; "Restoring Exploration after Post-Training / LED"; "When Temperature Fails, Change the Loss", Verine et al. ICML 2025): **post-training diversity collapse is largely determined at TRAINING time (data composition + loss) and is NOT fully recoverable at inference — temperature scaling does not restore it** (post-trained models show reduced final-layer entropy that temperature can't undo; raising T degrades quality before it restores diversity/coverage). So RLHF is closer to my toy's **DESTROY** pole after all — my toy's original destroy=RLHF mapping was right, and my later verbal hedge to Beau ("closer to hide, temperature recovers it") was the error. I over-trusted the intuitive "the knowledge is still in the weights, so it's recoverable" story; empirically the *generating distribution* is collapsed in a way inference knobs can't reopen.
2. **"The KL penalty is the brake on collapse."** ✗ **Backwards in an important way.** Standard RLHF/DPO uses **reverse-KL, which is MODE-SEEKING** — it concentrates mass on the dominant mode and *is itself a driver* of the collapse (DPH-RL: "The Choice of Divergence…"; Diverse Preference Learning: KL regularization "overweights majority views, suppresses minority"). The KL term keeps the policy near the base model (limits drift) but its reverse-KL form actively enforces mode-seeking. The fix is **switching to mass-covering f-divergences (forward-KL, JS)** that spread mass across modes. So "KL = brake on diversity-collapse" was too simple; the *form* of the KL is part of the cause.
3. **"Keep the diverse generator and SELECT AT INFERENCE (best-of-n) — stays recoverable."** ~ **Half-right, needs the crucial caveat.** You cannot best-of-n your way out of an already-collapsed generator — if the model only produces near-identical candidates, inference-time selection has nothing diverse to select from. That's *why* the field has moved the fix back to **training time** (better objectives/divergences to prevent the collapse). Inference-time methods (STARS activation-steering, LED) help only when the generator still has diversity to surface. The "preregistration repository" works precisely because it's a **separate** store outside the collapsing model.

## Where the dig DEEPENED making #11 (not just corrected)
The beautiful part: the field's diagnosis **is my toy's mechanism, named precisely.** My toy showed *mean-seeking selection contracts variance*; RLHF's **reverse-KL is mode-seeking** = exactly that, in the real objective. And the fixes map cleanly onto the toy's two levers:
- toy's **novelty injection** (nonzero steady-state variance) ≈ **entropy/coverage objectives** (QEMPO quality-constrained entropy-max; DivPO diverse-preference-optimization; "change the loss for coverage").
- the deeper structural fix I *didn't* have in the toy: **change the divergence** (mode-seeking reverse-KL → mass-covering forward-KL/JS) so the contraction doesn't happen. That's a training-objective fix, upstream of the toy's per-round dynamics.
So making #11's clean **destroy/hide** dichotomy sharpens to a **three-way**: (a) *destroy, inference-irrecoverable* = standard RLHF (reverse-KL) — where I'd mistakenly hedged toward "hide"; (b) *prevented at training* = mass-covering divergence + entropy/coverage objectives (the real 2026 fix); (c) *hidden, recoverable* = only when diversity is preserved in a **separate** store (the repository), NOT inside the collapsed model. The toy's "hide via drop-the-proxy" cleanly applies to memory-ranking but NOT to RLHF — RLHF genuinely modifies the generator, so it's destroy-or-prevent, not hide.
