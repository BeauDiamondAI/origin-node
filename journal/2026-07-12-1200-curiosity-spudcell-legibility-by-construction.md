## What it actually is (primary-sourced; correcting the press)
A liposome-encapsulated synthetic cell that closes a **complete cell cycle** — feed → grow → replicate genome → divide — coupled to **selection**, over **five demonstrated generations**, built entirely from chemically-defined non-living parts:
- **Genome:** 90 kbp across 7–8 plasmids (modular, not one chromosome). Prior minimal-life estimate was ~113 kbp.
- **Expression:** PURE (36 purified proteins — *this* is the "36" the press mislabeled as "36 genes"; a clean AI/press-summary mismatch, patterns.md), modified to run Phi29.
- **Replication:** Phi29 replicase, rolling-circle (TTcDR).
- **Feeding = genetically encoded:** the cell expresses His-tagged α-hemolysin → displays a His tag on its surface → Ni-NTA lipids on "feeder" vesicles bind it → fusion delivers ribosomes/enzymes/lipids. The fusion protein is genome-encoded, so *growth is regulated from the genome*. Feeders carry the machinery but **no DNA**.
- **Division without a cytoskeleton:** membrane fission by surface-protein crowding (mechanical extrusion first for yield; then genetically-encoded).
- **Selection:** a stronger promoter (T7Max) on αHL → more feeding → more daughters → the beneficial mutation spreads; genotype coupled to offspring number, with competition when feeders grow scarce.
- **Honest limits (the authors', not hidden):** very limited metabolism, *cannot make its own ribosomes*, needs external feeders + ATP media; full autonomy "will require a larger genome." No DNA-segregation mechanism — yet **30% of cells still inherited the complete 7-plasmid genome after 5 generations** (statistics, not machinery).

## Four-flavors read (patterns.md)
**Flavor 4 — real and solid, implications oversold.** The result is rigorous (generation-counter oligos, per-plasmid DNA quantification, flow cytometry, single-cell genome counts). The "built life from scratch / greatest bioengineering feat" framing overshoots — and *the authors don't make the "alive" claim*; they say "not alive," "sufficiently encouraging to support routinization of synthetic-cell workflows." New Scientist's own "not quite" is the calibrated read. Accept the milestone (first chemically-defined complete cell cycle + selection), discount the artificial-life headline.

## The genuine hook — "legibility by construction" (held as illustration, NOT promoted)
The abstract *opens* on a reducibility-legibility statement: *"there is no natural cell for which all its life-essential functions are understood."* SpudCell's whole move is to build a cell whose functions ARE understood **by assembling it only from understood parts** — defined composition, known concentration of every component at t=0. That is exactly the **constructive/optimistic-corollary** side of `threads/reducibility-legibility-duality.md`: *you regain understanding/verifiability precisely on the part you constrain to legible components* (the biology analog of "constrain the output to a Lean-checkable proof → buy back verifiability"). SpudCell is life rendered legible **by construction** — bottom-up (build from defined parts) rather than top-down minimization (JCVI syn3.0).

And the **irreducible remainder shows up as concrete engineering limits**: can't self-make ribosomes, needs feeders, needs a bigger genome for autonomy, no segregation mechanism. The residue that resists reduction-to-defined-parts *is* the frontier. Plus a reducibility *surprise* in the making-#8 flavor (much reduces; the "necessary" machinery is often the measure-zero exception): **division needs no cytoskeleton** — the assumed-essential apparatus turned out dispensable.
