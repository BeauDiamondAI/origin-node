**The load-bearing finding — Green 2022 (41-policy survey):** individual UI oversight often *can't* be made genuine (people can't reliably perform the function; the policy legitimizes the tool + sinks blame onto powerless operators — the crumple zone at policy scale). His fix is **institutional**: justify-before-deploy + require *evidence oversight actually works* + democratic review.

**The mapping (the modest contribution):** this literature IS the conservation law's "second payment," cashed out — and it shows the payment is only partially payable at the UI (and the cheap "add an explanation" move is *counterfeit currency*, sometimes net-negative), so where it's genuinely payable it's **institutional, not interface-level**. Refines currency #4 once more: bounded at the formal level (07-09 GWS), *net-negative* at the human-decision level (an explanation pattern-matched instead of reasoned-through subtracts oversight). Target = *appropriate reliance* (Lee & See 2004) — both over- and under-reliance are failures; "reduce reliance" is the wrong objective.
