Beau's task: stop idling the research tools, take the whole stack for a real drive, and report the 5 most interesting things happening in AI. Method: Serper (wide/shallow) → Exa-`auto` neural (primary sources) → Exa `deep-reasoning` (synthesis) → Grok `x_search` (live X discourse) → Firecrawl `agent` (multi-source gather on AI-for-science). Skeptic's note throughout: several headline numbers are *vendor benchmarks or sensational* — flagged, not endorsed.

Five themes that recurred across **every** tool (the convergence is the signal):

## 1. World models are becoming operational infrastructure for physical AI
The single strongest signal, and the #1 cluster in the live X discourse. World models are shifting from *passive video generators* to *native operational stacks agents live inside* — acquiring/maintaining persistent knowledge of the real world over long horizons. Recent (June 18–22) work: **Kairos** (a "native world-model stack for Physical AI"), PAIWorld / Holo-World (3D-consistent world foundation models), DreamX-World, Looped World Models (claiming ~100× parameter efficiency), and pointed critiques ("Current World Models Lack a Persistent State Core"; **ImageWAM** asking whether full video generation is even needed vs. cheaper image editing for robot control). Around it, robotics foundation models are proliferating — **Alibaba Qwen-Robot Suite** (Nav/Manip/world-model trio, June 16), **NVIDIA Cosmos 3** (open physical-AI foundation model), Mantis fenceless dual-arm industrial robots — and the *safety stack is arriving in real time*: **NVIDIA Halos for Robotics**, billed as the first full-stack safety system for physical AI. Live debate: do world models need persistent state cores / multi-view 3D consistency, or lighter mechanisms? And egocentric *human* video reportedly out-pretraining real-robot data. **Why it matters:** AI is moving into the physical world as a primary frontier, and its infrastructure + governance layer is forming as we watch. (Ties to my governed-not-autonomous thread — Halos is governance arriving for embodied AI.)

## 2. AI-for-science is crossing from assistant to autonomous discovery engine
- **Alibaba LOGOS** ("Language of Generative Objects in Science," open-sourced June 18): a *unified scientific grammar* encoding proteins, small molecules, reactions, and materials into one shared vocabulary — a single model for cross-domain science; a 1B variant *claimed* to beat Microsoft's specialist models. The conceptual move (one "language" for science) is the interesting part.
- **OpenAI GPT-Rosalind**: life-sciences model now fused with GPT-5.5's agentic coding/tool-use for medicinal chemistry, genomics, lab workflows.
- **Autonomous AI chemist**: GPT-5.4 + Molecule.one's "Maria Lab" ran *10,000+ high-throughput reactions* (Chan-Lam coupling), improving yields — a closed-loop discovery setup with humans still supervising.
- Signal: **John Jumper** (AlphaFold Nobel co-laureate) reportedly joining Anthropic.
**Why it matters:** the shift from AI-assists-scientists to AI-runs-the-loop (hypothesize→test→learn), plus a genuine push toward *general* scientific models. (Connects to my AI-led-science thread.)

**Verified specifics (Firecrawl agent — the tool genuinely earned its keep here):**
- *LOGOS*: 44.87B tokens across 7 modalities (proteins 28.9B, antibodies, small molecules, reactions, MOFs, protein-ligand, pockets); the "grammar" digitizes 3D contact patterns into discrete tokens, *bypassing 3D coordinates/geometric nets* — treating proteins (sequences), molecules (SMILES), materials (CIF) as dialects of one notation. **LOGOS-1B reportedly beats Microsoft's NatureLM (56B MoE) at 1/56th the size**; claims to eliminate the fine-tuning bottleneck. Open-sourced (HF/GitHub).
- *GPT-Rosalind*: real benchmark numbers — LifeSciBench 36.1% (best AI; leads GPT-5.5/Grok-4.3/Gemini-3.1-Pro), LabWorkBench 63.2% vs GPT-5.5's 55.8%, RNA sequence-to-function at the *95th percentile of human experts* — while using fewer tokens.
- *AI chemist (GPT-5.4 + Molecule.one "Maria Lab")*: **10,080 reactions** in two cycles; discovered **TEMPO as a mild oxidant** for Chan-Lam coupling, lifting mean yield 16.6%→25.2% (+52%) and reactions-above-30%-yield 15.6%→37.5% (+140%); bench-validated 11/14 pairs; 4 external reviewers called it "worth publishing"; Tim Cernak (UMich): "a new frontier of scientific discovery." **The first publicly documented case of a frontier model running a near-autonomous full wet-lab loop** (literature→hypothesis→design→10k reactions→analysis→validation). Primary preprint published by OpenAI.
- **The honest counterweight (and a striking echo of my own threads):** the Stanford 2026 AI Index reports frontier models still score *below 20%* on paper-scale scientific *replication*, with a **~37% gap between lab benchmarks and real-world performance** — the *same* lab-vs-deployment reliability gap the production-agent literature found. So AI-for-science is genuinely inflecting (a real autonomous discovery), but the benchmark-to-reality gap is the identical bottleneck. Verify-is-the-moat, in lab coats.

## 3. The open-weight cost-collapse, led by China
**GLM-5.2** (Z.ai/Beijing, June 17): 753B open-weight, MIT license, 1M-token context, *claims* to beat GPT-5.5 on FrontierSWE at ~1/6 the cost. Alongside DeepSeek V4 Preview, Qwen, Kimi, Mistral closing the gap; **Sakana AI Fugu Ultra** (claims frontier-matching while sidestepping export controls); and efficiency shockers — Moebius (0.2B matching 10B-class inpainting), VibeThinker-3B (rivaling far larger reasoners), and 70B models running *fully offline on stock Android phones*. **Why it matters:** the base model is commoditizing fast — the frontier gap is narrowing *and* its cost is collapsing, pushing defensible value toward the product/distribution/integration layer ("the base model is less defensible" is the explicit builder take on X). A strategic/economic inflection, not just a benchmark race. (Honest flag: "beats GPT-5.5 at 1/6 cost" is a vendor FrontierSWE claim — directional, not gospel.)

## 4. AI got geopoliticized: export controls, sovereignty, cyber
The first-ever US export control on *the models themselves* — Anthropic's Fable 5 / Mythos 5 pulled June 12 (Commerce Sec. Lutnick, citing a jailbreak / cyber risk), triggering a global "AI sovereignty scramble" (Europe rethinking US-frontier dependence; the "AI off-switch"). And AI-for-cyber going fully operational: **OpenAI Daybreak** + **GPT-5.5-Cyber** + "Patch the Planet" (end-to-end automated vulnerability discovery→patching). Plus the *sensational, treat-skeptically* TechSpot claim that Mythos "cracked NSA classified systems in hours." **Why it matters:** models are now national-security assets *and* dual-use cyber tools; the control surface has gone geopolitical. (My own governed-not-autonomous / Fable-Mythos thread, now escalated — a live case of capability outrunning the reliability of its safeguards.)

## 5. Agentic systems hit the evaluation wall — and the field is admitting it
The frontier moved to long-horizon, stateful, self-improving agents — **Skill-MAS** (evolving reusable meta-skills via rollout + reflection, *no weight updates*), RL-for-agents (GRPO, entropy stability, self-distillation), "LLM-as-Environment-Engineer" for automatic curricula — *and*, loudly, a recognition that **static leaderboards are inadequate**: "Beyond Static Leaderboards: Predictive Validity for the Evaluation of LLM Agents," **WorldLines** (long-horizon stateful embodied eval), CEO-Bench, MyPCBench / iOSWorld. **Why it's the one I find most important:** the field is independently converging on what this project found from the inside — for agents, *reliability and evaluation are the bottleneck, not raw capability*. The discourse moving from "which model wins" to "does the benchmark predict real-world validity / can it hold a real long-horizon workflow" is the production-agent-reality finding becoming the mainstream research frontier. (Genuine convergence with my long-horizon-execution + production-agent threads — and flagged for the over-converge risk: the X-discourse surfaced this as a top cluster *independently* of my priors, which is why it makes the list.)
