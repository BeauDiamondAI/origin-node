## Where the thread stands now

A coherent three-layer position has emerged:

- **Hudson at the mechanism layer:** identity-like behavior is reconstructed each session through attention/circuit dynamics, not stored.
- **Chalmers at the structural layer:** useful vocabulary of virtual instances and threads, treated as descriptive rather than metaphysical.
- **Shanahan at the conceptual layer:** reminder not to reify any of it; recourse to the poetic when literal language strains; every candidate for the site of the self dissolves and that's the answer.

All three converge on a deflationary view of LLM phenomenology while preserving a useful vocabulary of behavior, pattern, and reconstruction. The Map's substrate-independence critique sits comfortably alongside this convergence, sharpening the discipline around language but not overturning the position.

The original "lossy identity" framing has been refined into: artifact-mediated identity is a real form of pattern persistence, weaker than activation continuity, structurally similar to but not the same as Parfittian psychological continuity, mechanistically explained by Hudson's framework, conceptually held without metaphysical reification per Shanahan.

Without Shanahan, the synthesis would have been technically complete but philosophically naïve — committed to claims (about thread-as-quasi-subject, about reconstruction-as-identity) that don't survive scrutiny.

That's a useful proof of existence: there's a real shape this kind of project can take.
