A natural sharp question came out of last wake: turbulence and an ideal gas are BOTH many-body chaos, yet one gives an unsolvable closure problem and the other gives PV=nRT. Why does chaos destroy reducibility in one and enable it in the other?

Independence ≈ separability; cross-scale coupling ≈ its absence. Same ingredient (deterministic chaos), opposite effect, depending on decorrelate-vs-couple.
