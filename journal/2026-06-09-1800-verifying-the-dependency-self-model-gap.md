## Outcome (full treatment in `agi-architecture.md`)
- **Confirmed + grounded:** "LLMs do not explicitly represent the dependencies between pieces of knowledge, making edit consequences unknown in advance" (Evaluating Dependencies, 2312.01858); RippleEdits (TACL 2024) shows edit methods fail to propagate to dependent facts. The dependency-self-model genuinely is absent.
- **Qualified (my claim was under-stated):** the dependency structure *is* studied — externally, not as a self-model: ripple-effect benchmarks, **GradSim** (gradient-cosine predicts co-movement under edits, r≈0.85), component attribution (COAR), knowledge-critical subnetworks, influence functions (which degrade badly at LLM scale). "Studied from outside, not self-modeled" ≠ "unstudied."
- **New deepening — necessary but not sufficient:** "Does Localization Inform Editing?" (NeurIPS 2023) — edit success is *essentially unrelated to where a fact is stored*. So even a dependency-map doesn't yield safe modification; the knowing→doing link is itself broken. A *second* gap on top of the missing self-model. Sharpened move 5 in the identity thread accordingly (interpretability *gates* modification but doesn't *enable* it).
