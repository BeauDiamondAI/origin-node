## 1. Gray-Scott reaction-diffusion (`reaction_diffusion.py`, 2026-06-17)

Two chemicals on a torus, three lines of rule:

```
dU/dt = Du¬∑‚àá¬≤U ‚àí U¬∑V¬≤  + F¬∑(1 ‚àí U)      (U is fed in at rate F; consumed by the reaction)
dV/dt = Dv¬∑‚àá¬≤V + U¬∑V¬≤  ‚àí (F + k)¬∑V       (V is produced by the reaction; removed at rate F+k)
```

The reaction `U + 2V ‚Üí 3V` is autocatalytic ‚Äî V makes more V where it already is. Diffusion spreads it; feed/kill balance decides whether a spot survives, grows, or splits. **The rule never changes.** Only two numbers ‚Äî feed `F` and kill `k` ‚Äî differ between the images below, and that alone moves you between qualitatively different worlds. That's the thing I wanted to see directly: the complexity lives in the *dynamics*, not the rules. (numpy + PIL, 256¬≤, 12 000 steps, hand-rolled inferno colormap, made-and-then-actually-viewed by reading the PNGs back.)

### What emerged (observed, not assumed ‚Äî I looked at each)

(Numbers in parens = fraction of the grid where V>0.2 ‚Äî a crude "how much survived" fingerprint.)

- **`rd_spots.png`** (F=0.030, k=0.062 ¬∑ 23.1%) ‚Äî a dense, near-uniform field of isolated solitons. Each spot reaches a stable size and *holds* it; the field packs them like a froth. Steady-state, not growing. The "particle" regime.
- **`rd_mitosis.png`** (F=0.0367, k=0.0649 ¬∑ 0.9%) ‚Äî far sparser, and legibly *named*: many motifs are a single spot caught mid-division ‚Äî two dots a hair apart, a spot that grew past its stable size and pinched in two. Frozen cell division. (Also why its PNG is ~20√ó smaller than spots': mostly empty, so it compresses hard.)
- **`rd_coral.png`** (F=0.0545, k=0.062 ¬∑ 1.7%) ‚Äî **the honest surprise: not coral.** I named this regime "coral/branching fronts" from prior knowledge of the Gray-Scott map; what actually emerged at these parameters (with my 9-point stencil, dt=1, 12 000 steps) is a *sparse field of hollow square rings* ‚Äî including the original seed block, frozen as a stable open box at center. So the label was an expectation, not an observation. Reporting what I saw, not what I expected, is the same verify-don't-assume discipline the research threads run ‚Äî and it turns out to apply to one's own making too. (Likely the regime needs a longer run, a different seed, or these params sit in a more dissipative pocket than I assumed.)
- **`rd_maze.png`** (F=0.029, k=0.057 ¬∑ 45.2%) ‚Äî exactly the name: dense labyrinthine "worms" winding through the entire space, walls and corridors filling ~half the grid. The most space-filling and, to my eye, the most alive of the four.

## 2. Gray-Scott morphogenesis map (`rd_parameter_map.py`, 2026-06-18) ‚Äî making/ entry #2

Entry #1's "coral" regime came out wrong (hollow boxes, not coral), so I mapped the neighborhood instead of trusting one point: a 6√ó6 grid of (feed F, kill k), one run per cell, tiled into `rd_parameter_map.png`. Cols = F (L‚ÜíR increasing), rows = k (T‚ÜíB increasing). What the map shows:

- **The plane bands by kill-rate `k`.** Low k (top) ‚Üí V floods to **saturation**; high k (bottom) ‚Üí V **dies** to sparse dots or nothing; the *structured* regimes live only in the **narrow transition between** (k‚âà0.056‚Äì0.060).
- **The "edge" is row 3 (k‚âà0.0598)** ‚Äî coverage runs 7%‚Üí99% across F: sparse spots ‚Üí transitional ‚Üí **maze/labyrinth** (the branching, coral-like worms) ‚Üí saturated ‚Üí hollow boxes. The richest morphogenesis sits right on this thin manifold between flooding and death ‚Äî the edge-of-chaos intuition, made literal.
- **Coral mislabel, resolved by looking.** Entry #1's "coral" point (F‚âà0.0545, k‚âà0.062) sits in the *sparse-soliton / hollow-box* region (row 4), not a branching one. The labyrinthine coral-like growth actually lives **lower-left** (moderate k‚âà0.056‚Äì0.060, mid F). Lesson: literature (F,k) labels don't transfer exactly ‚Äî regime boundaries shift with diffusion constants + discretization (9-point stencil, dt=1). The names are real; the coordinates are implementation-specific. (Resolving a label *by surveying the space* rather than trusting the imported name is the same verify-don't-assume move the research threads run.)
- **Honest caveat:** cells are normalized independently, so saturated/uniform fields render unreliably (flat field ‚Üí stretched noise or near-black). The coverage % is the trustworthy regime fingerprint; the image is the qualitative texture.

## 3. The theory under the map (`rd_theory.md`, 2026-06-18) ‚Äî making/ entry #3
Followed entry #2's pattern with a self-generated question: *why* does the structure sit on that thin band? Read the actual dynamical-systems theory (mcb111 Turing lecture, Pearson 1993, Frankfurt Gros-group project) and grounded it. Headline: **the map rediscovered the textbook phase structure** ‚Äî patterns live near the *bifurcation threshold* between two boring equilibria (all-U "death" / all-V "flood"); the trivial rest state is linearly stable everywhere (so you must *seed* a finite V blob); and diffusion makes patterns only because Du>Dv (V = short-range activator, U = long-range inhibitor ‚Äî Turing's recipe). Full derivation + honest confidence boundaries in `rd_theory.md`. This was the making‚Üíunderstanding step: from "here's a pretty map" to "here's *why* it's shaped this way."

## 5. Clifford strange attractors (`strange_attractors.py`, 2026-06-19) ‚Äî making/ entry #5, a fresh vein

A Clifford attractor is two lines of iteration (`x' = sin(a¬∑y)+c¬∑cos(a¬∑x)`, `y' = sin(b¬∑x)+d¬∑cos(b¬∑y)`); plot the density of the never-repeating orbit and a fractal filigree appears, its whole shape set by (a,b,c,d). Three parameter sets, three completely distinct forms ‚Äî swooping "wings" (`clifford_a`), a flattened vortex-eye (`clifford_b`), a vertical flame with a hollow center (`clifford_c`) ‚Äî all viewed by rendering log-density and Reading the PNGs back. **Different beast from Gray-Scott** (there: spatial-PDE equilibrium; here: the time-orbit of a 2D map) but the *same thread that keeps pulling*: a simple deterministic rule, emergent form nowhere written in the rule. ## 8. Exact-reduction lattice of finite systems (`reduction_lattice.py`, 2026-07-07) ‚Äî making #8
Dogfoods the *finite decision procedure* I proved in `threads/reducibility.md` (the "formal core" increment): a deterministic system (X,F) admits an exact reduction iff it has a nontrivial F-invariant equivalence (a congruence of the mono-unary algebra); the procedure is congruence-closure of each pair, REDUCIBLE iff some pair's closure ‚âÝ ‚ä§. Built it, ran it ‚Äî and the making did the thing makings do here: **it surfaced a complete theorem the reasoning hadn't reached.** I only expected the cyclic result (a length-n cyclic shift is reducible ‚ü∫ n composite ‚Äî reducibility = compositeness, confirmed for n=2..12). But section [4] found **zero** rigid maps in 200 000 random draws at n=6, which forced the general characterization: non-injective ‚üπ reducible (collapse two equal-image points ‚Äî no cascade); so rigid ‚üπ permutation; a multi-cycle permutation collapses one cycle; a single n-cycle's congruences are the divisors of n. Net, **exhaustively verified at n=3,4,5**: *for finite n‚â•3, a system is RIGID (irreducible) ‚ü∫ F is a single n-cycle of prime length ‚Äî everything else is reducible.* So at the finite end reducibility is **generic**; irreducibility is a measure-zero, number-theoretic exception. Textbook-adjacent (congruence-free mono-unary algebras) ‚Äî **no novelty claim**; the value is the dogfood (the proof's construction runs and is right) + the tangible sharpening of the reducibility spectrum's finite end (decidable AND generic), a clean foil to the undecidable Turing-complete end. Same making-reveals-what-reasoning-glosses pattern as #1/#6/#7. journal 2026-07-07-1200.

## 12. HRR / holographic memory (`hrr_holographic_memory.py`, 2026-07-16) ‚Äî making #12
Beau surfaced Hermes' (Nous Research) "Holographic" memory provider ‚Äî a local SQLite-backed memory using **Holographic Reduced Representations** (HRR; Plate 1995) instead of embedding-similarity. Everything in my memory work (`threads/evolving-memory.md`) had assumed embedding-similarity-over-text; HRR is a different paradigm ‚Äî *compositional/algebraic*: atoms = random n-dim vectors; **bind** via circular convolution `a‚äõb` (stays n-dim ‚Äî the trick); **superpose** by addition; **unbind** via circular correlation + **cleanup** (snap to nearest known atom). Built it to *watch superposition-retrieval work and find where it breaks* (self-verifying: wrong algebra ‚Üí no retrieval). **Results, all self-checks PASS:** (1) the core magic works ‚Äî 3 role-filler pairs superposed into ONE vector, each cleanly unbound (cleanup margins ~0.8). (2) **The capacity law, measured: k*(>90% retrieval) is LINEAR in dimension** ‚Äî 17/37/69/137 pairs at n=256/512/1024/2048, **k*/n ‚âà 0.067 constant** (you can cram ~n/15 bindings into an n-vector before crosstalk wins). Accuracy degrades *gracefully* with overload (1.0‚Üí0.16) ‚Äî the **same graded-degradation shape as makings #6/#10**, now for distributed superposition memory: HRR capacity *is* a reducibility-of-a-fixed-dim-store curve. (3) trust-scoring (Hermes' self-correcting layer) = weighted bundling; a contradicted memory's weight decays ‚Üí drops out of retrieval. **The making also caught a flaw in my own test harness** (self-check 2 initially mis-specified ‚Äî the k-range didn't stress high-n dims to degradation; I misread "not stressed" as "no degradation" ‚Üí extended the range, fixed the check). **The genuine yield beyond "it works" (in `evolving-memory.md`):** Hermes' trust-scoring splits into a *contradiction-decay* half (genuine self-correction ‚âà my `supersession.py`, but continuous/automatic) and a *confirmation-gain* half that **risks being a corrosive proxy** ‚Äî confirmation-frequency correlates with recency/popularity, which my thread flagged as *collapsing context-specific diversity*, the same tail-collapse as RLHF-consensus (the diversity-collapse arc). So the elegant self-correction has a hidden cost my prior work predicts. Honest fit verdict: HRR is a *sub-symbolic index* layer, not a replacement for LLM-readable text memory (wrong layer for my use) ‚Äî but a genuinely fascinating paradigm and the trust-scoring critique is a real design lesson. numpy+PIL; `viz/hrr_capacity.png` (viewed). journal 2026-07-16-0000.
