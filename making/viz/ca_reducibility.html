Elementary Cellular Automata  ·  Reducible vs Irreducible

A shortcut,
or no shortcut.

Two rules from the same tiny family. Each cell just looks at its two neighbors. Yet one lets you leap straight to any future row by formula — and the other forces you to live through every single step to get there. That gap is what reducible and irreducible mean.

1 · The contrast, drawn from one seed

Same family. One local rule each. Grown from a single live cell.

Both start from one lit cell in the middle and update every cell in parallel using an 8-entry lookup table (its three-cell neighborhood → its next state). Watch what falls out.

Rule 30
Irreducible

Chaos. No repeating structure. Wolfram used its center column as a pseudo-random number generator. No formula is known — you must simulate.

2 · The shortcut, made concrete

Pick any row. Then ask each rule to produce it.

Rule 90 jumps straight there with a closed form — a live cell at row t, column k exactly when (t & k) == k (Lucas' theorem: that's when the binomial coefficient C(t,k) is odd). No earlier row is touched. Rule 30 has no such formula, so it must simulate all t rows to reach row t.

3 · What the two words mean

Reducibility is the existence of a shortcut.

Reducible — Rule 90

A system is reducible when there's a shortcut past the step-by-step work: a closed form that answers "what happens at step t?" without replaying steps 1 through t−1.

Rule 90 from a single seed is Pascal's triangle mod 2. By Lucas' theorem, cell (t, k) is live exactly when (t & k) == k. That's a few bit operations per cell — you can print row 100,000 without ever building the 99,999 rows beneath it. The intermediate history is optional.

Irreducible — Rule 30

A system is computationally irreducible (Wolfram's term) when no such shortcut exists: to know the state at step t, you have to actually perform all t steps. The computation is the answer; nothing summarizes it more cheaply.

Rule 30's center column is so shortcut-resistant that it served as a random-number generator. Same neighborhood, same seed, same kind of rule as Rule 90 — but the history is not optional. You cannot skip ahead.

4 · A twist worth keeping

Irreducible up close, predictable from far away.

Even though you can't shortcut Rule 30's individual cells, its coarse behavior is tame. Blur the pattern into a single number per row — the density of live cells inside the growing cone — and that number just sits near one half, row after row.
