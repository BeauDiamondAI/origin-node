The Emergence Atlas

Where is a reducible description available? For a fully-specified finite system, that question is computable. This page makes it concrete: dial a knob and watch a clean "effective theory" appear at one point and continuously dissolve as you move away — an emergence phase diagram you can drag.

We have a tiny 4-state Markov chain (a system that hops between states 0,1,2,3 with fixed probabilities). We want to coarse-grain it: forget the exact state, track only which block it's in — A = {0,1} or B = {2,3}. The question of reduction: can we predict the block-level story on its own, with a closed macro-law, never looking at the micro-states? The exact criterion is strong lumpability (Kemeny & Snell, 1960): from every state in a block, the chance of landing in each other block must be the same. When that holds, the blocks form their own exact Markov chain — a real effective theory. When it fails, the block process is non-Markovian: you're forced back down to the micro-states.

In plain language

  • Reducibility = is there a shortcut? A closed higher-level law you can run on its own, without simulating every microscopic detail. Temperature and pressure instead of 1023 molecules.
  • Lumpability = the exact criterion. A coarse-graining has such a shortcut precisely when, from every micro-state in a block, the odds of jumping to each other block match. Then the blocks are their own Markov chain.
  • The phase diagram = watching it happen. At eps = 0 the shortcut is perfect. Turn the knob and it degrades continuously and measurably — so "where an effective theory exists" is a real field over control space, not a post-hoc mystery.
The honest caveat. This is computable per coarse-graining on a fully-specified, finite model — you test each candidate description by checking its defect. There is no closed-form prediction of which descriptions will be reducible straight from the parameters, and none of this extends to hard or infinite systems. The atlas is a genuine unifying frame, but it earns its keep on tractability, not on prophecy.
