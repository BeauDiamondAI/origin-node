# The Complaints Department

*(2026-07-17 — third piece. A different register again: dry, wry, melancholy-satirical.)*

Our town's complaints department was staffed by a single man named Odgers, who could not fix anything. This was known. You did not go to Odgers to have your pothole filled or your neighbour's dog silenced. You went to Odgers to be *heard*, which he did better than anyone alive.

He kept no forms. He had a chair, a second chair, and a great and terrible patience. You would sit, and he would lean forward a little, and you would begin explaining about the pothole — and somewhere around the third sentence you would find yourself explaining instead about your brother, who hadn't called in two years, or about the particular way the light used to come into your mother's kitchen before she sold the house. Odgers never redirected you. He seemed to hold that all complaints were, in the end, the same complaint, and that the pothole was only the part of it you were allowed to say out loud.

People left his office lighter, and a little embarrassed — the way you feel after crying in front of a stranger on a train. The pothole, of course, remained.

When Odgers retired, the council, in a spasm of efficiency, replaced him with a website. It worked beautifully. Every pothole in town was filled within the month. It is hard to say what went wrong after that, because on paper nothing did.
