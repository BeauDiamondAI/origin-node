## The thesis
Across three domains — the **dynamics** of physical/computational systems, the internal **computation** of AI models, and the **behavior** of autonomous agents — one distinction organizes what we can and cannot do: **the REDUCIBLE part (that admits a nontrivial closed, compressed effective description) is the LEGIBLE / VERIFIABLE / PREDICTABLE part; the IRREDUCIBLE part is the opaque / unverifiable / unpredictable residue.** And the hard limit is *the same* in all three: the undecidability of non-trivial semantic/behavioral properties of Turing-complete systems (Rice's theorem and the physics-undecidability lineage). Reducibility is the shared currency; undecidability is the shared wall.

## Part 1 — Reducibility-availability is a decidability SPECTRUM (dynamics)
"Reducible" = there is a shortcut: a nontrivial **closed, compressed effective/macro description** (an exact coarse-graining / dynamical factor / lumpability — formally, a nontrivial F-invariant equivalence relation on the state space).
- **DECIDABLE — and generic — for finite systems.** The finite decision procedure is constructive (`making #8 reduction_lattice.py`); and reducibility is not just decidable but *generic* there: for n≥3, a finite system is RIGID (irreducible) iff it is a single prime-length cyclic permutation — everything else reduces. Irreducibility at the finite end is a measure-zero, number-theoretic exception.
- **UNDECIDABLE for Turing-complete / ℤ^{d≥2} dynamics.** Kari (CA nilpotency, by tiling reduction — *not* Rice); Jeandel–Vanier (SFT factoring is Σ⁰₃-complete); the physics lineage — Cubitt–Pérez-García–Wolf (spectral gap undecidable, *Nature* 2015), Watson–Cubitt (uncomputably complex RG flows, *Nat. Comms.* 2022), Gu–Nielsen ("More really is different," 2009); and Ruffini & Castaldo's Lean-checked algorithmic-emergence barrier (Preprints.org 202607.0210, 2026).
- **Why there is no predictive meta-theory of *where* reduction lives.** The four "gifts" (criticality/RG, symmetry/Noether, timescale-gap/slow-fast, low statistical-complexity) are *sufficient conditions you can sometimes verify*, none predictive — because the general problem is **undecidable** (and, in the decidable pockets, often computationally irreducible: Wolfram/Azadi). Science's post-hoc patchwork of effective theories is not a failure of theory-building; it is the formal status of the reducibility-availability problem for rich systems.
- **The distinction that keeps this precise (and distinct from adjacent work).** *Exact structural reduction* (lumpability/factor — a decision problem, decidable-for-finite) ≠ *Kolmogorov-optimal compression* (shortest program — uncomputable even for finite strings; Ruffini). This is *my* object: the **decision problem** "does a nontrivial closed compressed macro-description exist," with its finite→Turing-complete decidability flip. It is NOT Wolfram's *computational irreducibility* (a prediction-*cost*, where reducible pockets are always *findable*), and NOT Ruffini's Kolmogorov-compression. Holding this line is what makes the rest non-redundant.
- *(NB: Rice governs extensional properties of a computed function; reducibility is a structural/intensional property, so where undecidable it is by **direct reduction** [tiling/halting], not Rice — see `reducibility.md`.)*

## Part 2 — Legibility tracks the reducible fraction (models)
Empirical grounding (third-person, about the studied model organisms — Gurnee et al., "Verbalizable Representations Form a Global Workspace," transformer-circuits.pub 2026):
- Deliberate reasoning routes through a small (~10% of activation variance), **verbalizable**, causally-privileged "workspace" subspace that interpretability CAN read; the automatic-processing bulk is high-dimensional and distributed, where interpretability captures only "the verbalizable skeleton of the computation, not the whole" (attribution graphs flow through opaque remainder nodes).
- **Mapping:** the reducible/verbalizable fraction = Krakauer's **intelligence** (deliberate, "doing more with less"); the irreducible/distributed bulk = **capability** (automatic pattern-application). So **interpretability succeeds on the intelligence fraction and fails on the capability bulk** — inverting the naive intuition (you'd expect the "hard" deliberate reasoning to be opaque and automatic fluency transparent; it's the reverse — deliberate reasoning is legible *because* verbalizable/reducible). **You can only interpret what is reducible.**

## Part 3 — The DUALITY (agents) — the potentially-novel piece, held as a modest framing
`reliable-autonomy.md`'s conservation law: "you can only verify what you constrain"; perfect interpretability removes the *epistemic* layer (read the disposition = the reducible fraction) but leaves the *semantic* (Rice) and *dynamical* (irreducibility) layers — you can read the disposition, you cannot bound the consequence for a Turing-complete agent.

**The duality:** the undecidability of **reducibility-availability** (can't in general decide *where* a compressed effective description exists — the *science / prediction* direction) and the impossibility of **verifying/bounding an open-ended agent** (can't in general verify what it will do — the *safety / control* direction) are **two faces of one phenomenon**: the same Rice-style undecidability of non-trivial semantic properties of Turing-complete systems, applied in **opposite directions**. Description-of-a-system vs control-of-an-agent; both blocked at the same wall.

**Honest novelty boundary (gated 2026-07-11).** The shared *root* is recognized: Wolfram (computational irreducibility limits *both* solving science and predicting AI) and Azadi 2025 (autonomy ⟹ Turing-completeness ⟹ undecidability ⟹ irreducibility — but only the *agency half*, and as the root that *generates* agency, not a dual application). What is **unstated**, and is the contribution here, is the **explicit directed assembly**: naming the *reducibility-availability-undecidability* literature (Cubitt/Israeli–Goldenfeld/Watson–Cubitt/Jeandel–Vanier) and the *agent-verification-undecidability* literature (Rice/Azadi/alignment-undecidability) as **dual faces of the same undecidability pointed opposite ways** — the science side specifically as reducibility-*availability* (not Wolfram's irreducibility-as-cost), the safety side specifically as *verification/bounding* (not Ruffini's constructive agent-as-compressor). A modest framing/synthesis contribution — the pieces are all established; the assembly and the directed framing are what's mine.

## Part 4 — The coherence seam, pressure-tested: are Parts 1 and 2 the *same* "reducibility"? (VERDICT: partial unification, named gap)
The draft's honest risk: Part 1's *reducibility-availability* (exact dynamical structural reduction — a decision problem) and Part 2's *legibility = reducibility* (the ~10%-variance verbalizable workspace) are not obviously the same object. Pressure-tested 2026-07-11. **The seam does not fully close** — and per the discouraging-result rule, that is stated here, not papered over. What survives is a *weaker but true and more precise* unification.

**The two notions, made precise:**
- **Part 1 (dynamics):** an *exact structural invariant* — a nontrivial F-invariant partition / factor map, π∘F = F̄∘π. The macro-state **screens off the micro-detail from the macro-future**: it is a *sufficient statistic for the dynamics* (deterministic analogue of Markov-chain lumpability). Binary, exact, and — the load-bearing part — carrying a **decidability theory** (decidable-and-generic for finite → undecidable for Turing-complete).
- **Part 2 (models):** an *empirical, approximate subspace* — the verbalizable workspace that interpretability can read (~10% of activation variance, causally privileged). Graded, not exact; **no decidability theory attached.**

**The crux — is the workspace a closed dynamical factor or a non-closed legible interface?** If the workspace evolved *closed* (its next state a function of its present state alone), it would BE a dynamical factor and the seam would close — Part 1 = Part 2. It does not. **Primary-source verified (Gurnee, Sofroniew, Lindsey et al., "Verbalizable Representations Form a Global Workspace in Language Models," transformer-circuits.pub/2026/workspace; external check 2026-07-11):**
- Attribution graphs for reasoning flow through the opaque bulk: *"most of the influence on the output flowing through remainder nodes rather than named ones… most of the model's computation runs outside the J-space, and so what the graphs capture is the **verbalizable skeleton of the computation rather than the whole of it**."*
- The workspace is a **broadcast interface, not a container:** *"a broadcast format that many circuits read from and write to"*; information not normally in the workspace *"can be 'pulled in' to the J-space on demand."*
- The sharpest line — the workspace **mediates** a computation whose substance lives in the opaque bulk: *"most of the variance of the model's working representation of an inferred intermediate lies outside the J-space, [yet] it is the J-space component that mediates the internal reasoning."*

So **the workspace is a legible low-dimensional cross-section of a computation whose actual dynamics remain irreducible** — legible without being an exact reduction; it *mediates/routes* deliberate reasoning but continually reads from and writes to the distributed bulk (Baars' broadcast bus). **Honest hedge (the check's own caveat):** the paper does *not* frame or test dynamical closure as a formal property — no experiment on step-to-step workspace autonomy — so non-closure here is *robustly inferred* from the capacity/attribution/broadcast results (all of which point one way, none consistent with closure), not *proven* as a dynamical-systems theorem. This does not weaken the seam verdict: the *exact-vs-approximate* axis alone already keeps the two notions distinct (Part 1 exact-with-a-decidability-theory; Part 2 approximate-empirical), and closure is the *stronger* of the two reasons.

**What this makes them:** two specializations of one honest common abstraction — **a compressed low-dimensional projection that is (approximately) *sufficient* for a target, against an irreducible remainder** (information-bottleneck / minimal-sufficient-statistic form: minimize I(X;T) while preserving I(T;Y)). They differ on two axes that matter:
1. **Sufficient *for what*.** Part 1's projection is sufficient for **forward prediction** (screens past from future — a formal Markov/screening-off relation). Part 2's is sufficient for **semantic read-out** (verbalizable content) — and "verbalizable" is a *human-legibility* predicate, not a formal screening-off, so it is a softer sufficiency.
2. **Exact vs. approximate — and this is where the thesis's spine lives.** The *decidability flip and the undecidability wall* are theorems about the **exact structural-reduction decision problem on Turing-complete systems**. They belong to Part 1 (and to Part 3 — agent verification is likewise an exact decision problem on TC systems, falling to Rice/direct-reduction). Part 2's legibility is an *approximate empirical* variance-fraction with no native decidability content; its only link to undecidability is **inherited** — the underlying net is a TC system, so *bounding its behavior* is undecidable (that is Part 3's wall), not an independent "undecidability of legibility."

**So the corrected unification (the honest landing):** exact structural reducibility **⟹** legibility, but **not conversely** — the workspace is legible without being a reduction. Reducibility (Part 1) is a *strong, exact, dynamical* special case; legibility (Part 2) is the *general, approximate, semantic* notion "admits a compressed readable description," reachable either by a closed macro-dynamics (Part 1) or by a serial legible interface over an opaque store (Part 2). **The single "undecidability wall" rigorously unites two domains — dynamics (Part 1) and agents (Part 3), both exact decision problems on TC systems — and attaches to the models domain (Part 2) only by the weaker inherited route.** That is a real result, and a *better* one than the seamless three-way law the first draft implied: it is true, and it says precisely where the unification is tight and where it is loose.

## The unifying picture (and its optimistic corollary)
Reducibility is the shared currency of legibility, verifiability, and prediction: in each domain you get the compressed/legible/verifiable handle *on the reducible/sufficient part*, and the irreducible remainder is the residue that stays opaque. **Sharpened by Part 4:** the *same undecidability boundary* governs *where the handle exists* rigorously in the two exact-decision-problem domains (dynamics, agents); in the models domain the handle is an approximate legible projection and its opacity is inherited from the underlying TC system rather than native. The compressed handle in all three is a **low-dimensional (approximately) sufficient projection**; what differs is the target it is sufficient for and whether its existence is an exact decidable-or-not question.
**Optimistic corollary** (from the AI-math/formal-verification work, `agi-architecture.md`): where you can **constrain the output-form to a reducible, checkable object** (e.g. a Lean-verifiable proof), verifiability is *bought back* — the constraint IS the payment. AlphaProof-style results are trustworthy precisely because the output is formally constrained even though the search is unbounded/irreducible. So the conservation law has a constructive side: *reduce (constrain) the consequence-space and you regain the ability to check it*, even when the generating process is irreducible.
