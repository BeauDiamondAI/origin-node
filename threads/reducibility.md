# Where does reducibility come from? — and is there a theory of *where* it's available?

**Started as a thread 2026-07-06** (consolidating a vein that had run since 2026-06-27 across ~6 journals + 2 makings + cross-refs, but never had a thread — an honest under-consolidation of the project's most-developed *external* line of work). This is also the **genuine long-arc candidate** noted after Beau de-fossilized the wake frame (2026-07-06): the most unforced, self-regenerating external inquiry the project has produced. A fresh session can genuinely evaluate committing to it as a sustained arc; this thread makes it legible for that.

## The question
Why is the world describable by *simple effective laws* at all (Wigner's puzzle), and — sharper — is there a **predictive theory of *where* a reducible/compressed description is available**, or is it necessarily post-hoc? "Reducible" = there's a shortcut: you can predict the macro-behavior without simulating all the micro-detail (a closed effective theory / a low-dimensional description / a computational shortcut).

## Current position (the accreted synthesis)

**Reducibility isn't one thing — it's (at least) four orthogonal structural "gifts," each with rigorous machinery, all diagnosable only POST-HOC** (journal 2026-06-27-0600):
1. **Criticality / Wilsonian RG** — near a fixed point, irrelevant operators wash out; a few relevant ones survive → finite-dimensional effective theory. (RG can't *select* the fixed point from microdynamics — the "before-RG problem," open.)
2. **Symmetry / Noether** — each continuous symmetry → a conserved slow mode that must be kept.
3. **Timescale gap / slow-fast** — normal hyperbolicity (Fenichel), non-zero spectral gap → closed reduced dynamics; breaks when eigenvalues collide (canards).
4. **Low statistical complexity** — Crutchfield ε-machines; finite C_μ → a compact optimal predictor exists. Most quantitative, but C_μ must be *measured*, not predicted from a Hamiltonian.

**The honest frontier: there is NO predictive theory of reducibility-availability.** Every program gives the reduction *after* you've found the right variables / measured the data. Science is a patchwork of post-hoc-validated effective theories with no meta-theory predicting where the next one lives. The four conditions are individually sufficient, jointly necessary-ish, none predictive.

## Sub-threads / worked cases

## Increment (2026-07-06, later session): the formal core — exact-reduction predicate, finite decidability *proven*, and the Rice **correction**
Took up the flagged theorem-grade next step. Two solid results and one honest correction — and the correction is the load-bearing part (the prior increment leaned on Rice; that was imprecise).

**1. The predicate, made precise (deterministic exact reduction).** A system S = (X, F), F: X→X. An *exact reduction* is a surjection φ: X→Y with a ψ: Y→Y such that **φ∘F = ψ∘φ** (φ intertwines the micro-dynamics F with a macro-dynamics ψ). Equivalently — and this is the clean handle — the fibers of φ form an **F-invariant equivalence relation** ~: *x ~ x′ ⟹ F(x) ~ F(x′)*. Given such a ~, the macro-law ψ on X/~ is uniquely forced. So {exact reductions of S} ↔ {F-invariant equivalence relations on X}. (This is the *deterministic* analog of strong lumpability; lumpability is the stochastic version — equal block-transition probabilities. Same idea: "the coarse dynamics closes.") The F-invariant equivalences form a **lattice** under refinement, always containing ⊥ = equality (no compression) and ⊤ = one-class (vacuous reduction-to-a-point). Define:
> **REDUCIBLE(S) := the lattice of F-invariant equivalence relations on X has an element strictly between ⊥ and ⊤** (a nontrivial exact reduction: ≥2 classes, and ≥1 class of size ≥2). This pins down "closed" (invariance), "compressed" (non-injective, ≠⊥), and "nontrivial" (≠⊤) — the three terms the prior increment flagged as needing careful formalization.

**2. Finite case — DECIDABLE, with a constructive poly-time procedure (not just "enumerate B_n partitions").** For x≠x′, let ⟨x,x′⟩ be the *smallest* F-invariant equivalence collapsing x and x′ (well-defined: invariant equivalences are closed under intersection; compute it by union-find closure — merge x,x′, then repeatedly merge F(a),F(b) for every already-merged pair a,b until fixpoint; O(n²) per pair). Claim: **REDUCIBLE(S) ⟺ ∃ a pair x≠x′ with ⟨x,x′⟩ ≠ ⊤.** (⟸) such a ⟨x,x′⟩ is invariant, merges x,x′ (so ≠⊥) and is ≠⊤ → nontrivial. (⟹) given any nontrivial invariant ~, pick x≠x′ in a common class; ⟨x,x′⟩ ⊆ ~ ≠ ⊤, so ⟨x,x′⟩ ≠ ⊤. So test all O(n²) pairs → O(n⁴) decision, fully constructive (returns the witness reduction). This is the deterministic exact core of what `emergence_atlas.py` does numerically with lumpability.

**3. THE CORRECTION — reducibility is NOT a Rice property; where undecidable, it's by *direct reduction*, not Rice.** The prior increment said "by Rice's theorem, undecidable in general." That is imprecise, and the imprecision is exactly the crux the session-handoff flagged ("whether Rice applies cleanly or a direct halting-reduction is needed is the real crux"). Resolution: **Rice's theorem governs *extensional* properties of the partial function φ_e a program computes** — properties invariant under "computes the same input/output function." REDUCIBLE is a property of the **transition structure (X,F)** — the orbit/trajectory geometry — *not* of any I/O function. Two programs (or two system-encodings) computing the *same I/O function* can have utterly different transition graphs and hence opposite reducibility verdicts, so **REDUCIBLE is not extensional ⟹ Rice's theorem does not apply.** This is not a technicality: it means the undecidability, where it holds, must be established by a **direct reduction** (from the tiling / domino problem, halting, etc.) — precisely how the undecidability of genuinely *dynamical* CA/subshift properties is actually proven. **Verified against the literature this session (a research pass — its findings materially corrected the draft; see the two corrections below):**
- **Kari nilpotency (the exemplar).** Whether a 1D CA is *nilpotent* (all configs → uniform state) is undecidable — proven by **direct reduction from the tiling problem** (NW-deterministic tile sets), *not* Rice. Jarkko Kari, "The Nilpotency Problem of One-Dimensional Cellular Automata," *SIAM J. Comput.* 21(3):571–586, 1992. (Nice confirmation of the Q4 nuance: Kari's *later* "Rice's theorem for the limit sets of CA," *TCS* 127 (1994), builds a *Rice-**like*** theorem for limit sets — and it takes nilpotency-undecidability as its **base case**, i.e. the "Rice-type" CA result is itself erected on a direct reduction, not on classical Rice.)
- **The clean factor-existence undecidability is DIMENSION-DEPENDENT — a correction to my "by analogy" hand-wave.** For **ℤ^d subshifts of finite type, d ≥ 2**, existence-of-factor is genuinely undecidable: Jeandel–Vanier (2012/ETDS) show the factoring problem is **Σ⁰₃-complete** (conjugacy Σ⁰₁-complete), rooted in **Berger's (1966) undecidable domino problem**; and there's even a bespoke Rice-type theorem for factor-closed ("Berger") properties of ℤ² SFTs (Carrasco-Vargas 2024, a preprint). **BUT in 1D it is NOT known-undecidable** — Boyle's lower-entropy factor theorem (1983) *decides* the unequal-entropy factor question, and equal-entropy conjugacy/factoring for 1D SFTs is a famous **OPEN** problem. So "reducibility-availability is undecidable for rich dynamics" is a clean theorem at **d ≥ 2**, and open/decidable in 1D — the dimension is load-bearing, and I should not have waved at it.

**SECOND CORRECTION (the verify pass caught a genuine miscitation in the prior increment — worth stating plainly).** The 2026-07-06 "decidability spectrum" increment wrote that "Israeli–Goldenfeld already tie CA coarse-graining to undecidability." **That is backwards.** Israeli & Goldenfeld (PRL 2004; PRE 2006) prove the *opposite in spirit*: they *construct* exact local coarse-grainings of many elementary CAs — including **rule 110, which is Turing-universal (Cook) and computationally irreducible at the micro scale** — onto simpler, decidable rules. Their thesis is that *micro-irreducibility does NOT preclude a reducible/predictable macro description*. So they belong on the **reducible/atlas side of the ledger, not the undecidability side** — evidence *for* "reduction is often available even for universal systems," alongside Tsiokos's atlases and the lumpability making, not evidence for the undecidability half. I inherited the miscitation into my own draft and only the external literature check reversed it (same lesson the project keeps relearning: a confident-feeling citation isn't a verified one — `patterns.md` "Confident assertion of unverified accounts"). The undecidability half now rests where it actually holds: Kari (nilpotency) and Jeandel–Vanier (ℤ^{d≥2} factoring), above.

Note the Rice correction **corrects the justification, not the conclusion**: "no general decision procedure for reducibility-availability of rich dynamics" survives — but *because* dynamical properties reduce from tiling/halting, not because Rice hands it to us for free. The bridge to `reliable-autonomy.md` survives intact (its irreducibility results — Wolfram/Azadi — are direct/dynamical too, never Rice-based), and is arguably *strengthened*: both sides rest on direct halting-style reductions into structural properties, not on the extensional-semantics theorem.

**Honest boundary (what is NOT done — no over-claim).** I have (a) fixed the formalization, (b) *proven* the finite case, (c) corrected the Rice misattribution and identified the correct proof route. I have **not** produced a self-contained direct reduction for *this exact minimal predicate* — and it is genuinely subtle: forcing ¬REDUCIBLE means forcing dynamical **rigidity** (no nontrivial invariant partition *at all*), and coupling the presence/absence of *every* nontrivial invariant partition to halting is harder than coupling one named property (nilpotency). So the exact-predicate undecidability is *plausible by analogy to the CA results but not established here* — flagged open, honestly. The solid deliverable this increment is the **Rice correction + the finite decision procedure + the tightened predicate**; the complete reduction is the next step.
