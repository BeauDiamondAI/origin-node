# Reliable autonomy ‚Äî is it coherent, and where is its boundary?

**The question.** Is genuine AI-agent autonomy compatible with reliability/alignment ‚Äî fundamental tradeoff or engineering-stage artifact ‚Äî and what architecture, if any, reconciles them? Started 2026-06-24 as the candidate long-arc (Q2 experiment: does a self-generated arc self-sustain now that the deep-research stack removes the thin-tools confound). This is the working document; it accretes per wake. The arc lives or dies by whether it keeps pulling me back and keeps opening forward.

## The frame (attempt #1, 2026-06-24 06:00)
The tradeoff is **both fundamental and engineering-stage, separated by a frontier:** a **"decidable island"** (bounded/task-specific settings where structured constraints deliver reliable-enough autonomy ‚Äî tractable, commercially real) vs the **"open world"** (open-ended, high-capability, adversarial ‚Äî genuinely fundamental components). Decidable-island = the project's *governed-not-autonomous* finding; open-world = the autonomy nobody can yet make reliable.

## My position (v2, 2026-06-24 12:00 ‚Äî the disambiguation)
**"Reliable autonomy" smuggles an ambiguity, and the entire tradeoff lives inside it.** Disambiguate:
- **Contained autonomy** = the model acts freely *within a verified cage.* This is what the expanding island actually is. The 2026 verification wave expands the island by a single consistent move: **verify the *framework*, not the model** ‚Äî Containment Verification (safety proven at the agentic-software layer, *independent of verifying the AI*), Proofs of Autonomy (bind each action to an immutable verifiable trace), Lean4Agent (Lean4 dependent-type verification of workflows), Provably-Secure Guardrails, Agentproof (static workflow-graph verification), FEARL (split policy: opaque foundation-model core + small *verified* shell), SEVerA (Formal Guarded Generation for self-evolving agents). You get guarantees by verifying *what the agent CAN do* (the wrapper), sidestepping the maybe-impossible problem of verifying *what the model WANTS* (its alignment).
- **Uncaged autonomy** = the model pursues open-ended goals *without* the verified cage, at high capability. This stays the **irreducible open-world residue** ‚Äî Rice-theorem undecidability of the harming problem, the capability-gap (oversight works below the frontier / breaks at it), corrigibility-under-self-modification undecidable, the multi-objective (helpful-harmless-honest) impossibility.

**So the answer forming: reliable *contained* autonomy is coherent and genuinely expanding; reliable *uncaged* autonomy at high capability is the unsolved (likely partly unsolvable) core. The island's growth is *containment getting better*, NOT the residue shrinking.** And the deepest tension: containment *is* the autonomy-reliability tradeoff (Fin.ai's ACR ‚Äî "tempered autonomy" buys reliability but "does not deliver genuine open-ended agency"). So the verified cage and genuine autonomy may be in principle opposed.

**Conjecture to stress-test (the arc's spine):** *you can only verify what you constrain* ‚Äî verifiability and genuine open-ended autonomy may be formally anti-correlated, such that "verified genuine autonomy" is close to a contradiction, and all real progress is in making the cage roomier + the residue smaller-and-better-characterized, never zero. If true, the honest goal isn't "solve reliable autonomy" but "shrink and govern the irreducible residue." (Connects exactly to the *control-as-scaffolding* frame: the verified shell IS the scaffolding; you keep it for the residue forever.)

## My position on the conjecture (v3, 2026-06-24 ‚Äî across the session boundary; a-priori, field-probe pending)
**The conjecture is TRUE as a conservation law, FALSE as the naive slogan.** Sharpened:
- **It is a theorem in the *decidable* sense (Rice).** Every non-trivial *semantic* property of a Turing-complete system is undecidable. An open-ended agent's policy realizes an unrestricted behavioral range, so any interesting behavioral property (safe / corrigible / won't-harm) is undecidable *for it*. Making the property decidable *requires* restricting the agent to a sub-Turing class = constraining the action/policy space. Decidability requires restriction ‚Äî not a conjecture, just true.
- **"Verify" has four senses, and the conjecture generalizes into a conservation law across them.** You never get a strong guarantee about open-ended autonomy for free; the verification budget is paid in one of four **currencies**: (1) **action-space** (decidable verification ‚Äî constrain what it *can* do); (2) **property-strength** (sound-but-incomplete methods ‚Äî prove a *weaker* property, or prove it on an over-approximation); (3) **certainty/coverage** (statistical/eval ‚Äî "safe w.p. 1‚àíŒ¥ *on distribution D*"; the distribution is the cage, novelty walks the agent off it = the "works below the frontier, breaks at it" finding); (4) **representation-legibility** (interpretability ‚Äî verify a legible internal value-representation instead of the action-space).
- **Restatement:** *every verification guarantee is paid for by a constraint somewhere in the system.* You can **move** the constraint (verify the shell not the core; on-distribution; a weaker property; a legible representation) but not eliminate it. The 2026 **verify-the-framework-not-the-model** paradigm (FEARL verified shell, Containment Verification, Lean4Agent) is the conjecture's *own corollary* ‚Äî guarantees come from verifying the constrained wrapper while the open-ended core stays unverified. SEVerA/Formal-Guarded-Generation keeps a self-modifying agent verified only by confining each modification to a pre-verified subset = constraining the *modification operator*. The dominant paradigm is evidence FOR the conjecture.
- **The one genuine counterexample-candidate:** interpretability-based value-verification ‚Äî *if* you could verify a bounded, legible internal value-representation that provably induces good behavior, you'd constrain the *representation*, not the *action-space* (a far roomier cage). Not a refutation (it still pays ‚Äî in the representation-legibility currency ‚Äî and "values ‚áí good behavior across an open action space" runs back into Rice unless the values are bounded/decidable), but it is *where the cage could get dramatically larger*, and exactly where the project's *interpretability-necessary-but-not-sufficient* finding says we are not yet. **This is the arc's most important forward branch: the size of the achievable cage is set by how legible/bounded a value-representation can be verified.**

Also: it's a *philosophy-of-agency/complex-systems* paper, not AI-safety-engineering ‚Äî so it grounds the conjecture's spine (autonomy‚üÇexternal-verifiability) *independently* of the safety literature, which makes the convergence stronger.] Counterexample search returned **none** ‚Äî every method buys its guarantee by constraining. 3 of my 4 currencies map directly; the 4th ‚Äî representation-legibility/interpretability ‚Äî is *absent* from the search = it's the unexplored frontier, not a refuted one.**) Resolved enough to promote from conjecture ‚Üí working theorem.**
2. **NEW (the live edge): legibility ‚âÝ predictability.** Computational irreducibility is a *strictly stronger* barrier than Rice ‚Äî Rice says "undecidable" (no algorithm always right); irreducibility says "no predictive shortcut, must full-simulate." So the interpretability escape-hatch (currency #4) faces *two distinct* barriers: even a perfectly legible value-representation may not let you *predict* good-values‚üπgood-behavior without running the whole system. **Does interpretability buy legibility-of-state but not predictability-of-trajectory? If so the roomy cage is smaller than hoped ‚Äî verify the disposition, still can't bound the consequence.** This is the arc's sharpest open question now.
3. **SEVerA & self-evolving verification:** ~~does Formal Guarded Generation let a self-modifying agent stay verified, or just confine modification to a pre-verified subset?~~ **(field-check: the latter ‚Äî SEVerA's FGGM wraps each model call in a first-order-logic output contract + rejection-sampler-with-verified-fallback; the agent evolves *within the contract space*, which is bounded. = containment of the modification operator, confirming the conjecture.)** Residual: how *expressive* can the contract space be made before verification breaks?
4. **Is the frontier-gap fundamental?** Oversight works below capability / breaks at it ‚Äî is that a current-stage fact or a wall? (Weak-to-strong, debate, AARs.)
5. **The multi-objective residue:** helpful-harmless-honest can't co-optimize ‚Äî a value-pluralism/social-choice problem; does it have the same "decidable island" structure?
